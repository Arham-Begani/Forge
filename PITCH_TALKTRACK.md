## **ANTICIPATED FOLLOW-UPS & ANSWERS**

### **Q: Why Gemini instead of GPT-4?**
> "Gemini has Agent Teams capability, extended thinking, and native web search. For multi-agent orchestration, it's the best. Plus, Gemini API pricing is 50% cheaper than OpenAI. That matters for our unit economics."

### **Q: What's your differentiation vs. Copy.ai or Jasper?**
> "They're fragmented. Copy.ai is text generation. Jasper is content marketing. We're integrated pre-launch automation. We're offering Research + Branding + Landing Page + Marketing + Feasibility all in one **context**: agents talk to each other, they see each other's work, output is better.

> Plus, they don't have live deployment or AI board feedback. We're going after a different problem: startup validation, not just copywriting."

### **Q: How do you handle accuracy? What if the market research is wrong?**
> "Great question. A few things:
> 1. Genesis Engine uses web_search tool. It cites sources, so you can verify.
> 2. Shadow Board specifically stress-tests research assumptions.
> 3. We're not claiming 100% accuracy. We're claiming 'better than blank page in 1 hour'. Founders use this as a starting point, not gospel.
> 4. Over time, we track which recommendations led to successful ventures. Feedback loop = better research."

### **Q: What's your take on data privacy? Are you storing user data?**
> "Yes. We store ventures, conversations, and generated outputs in a secure Postgres database (Antigravity). We have GDPR/CCPA compliance roadmap. Terms of Service is clear: we own the product, you own your venture data. We don't train on user content — Gemini API data is governed by Google's data policies.

> Enterprise customers get dedicated infrastructure + contractual guarantees."

### **Q: Isn't the founder experience just typing a prompt into a UI? What's the retention risk?**
> "It's more sticky than it looks:
> 1. **Venture objects are persistent.** Users come back to edit, iterate, refine. Each edit is data.
> 2. **Cohort mode comparison** locks you in. Once you've compared 3 variants, switching costs are high.
> 3. **Investor Kit + timeline** are collaborative tools. Teams use Forze together.
> 4. **Automation phase** (marketing publishing) makes it a weekly/daily tool, not a one-off.
> 5. **Data value increases over time.** The more runs you do, the better the system knows your venture.

> Retention is a risk we're managing. But beta users are doing 5-7 runs per venture, not 1. That's sticky."

### **Q: What's your competition?**
> "Head-to-head: ChatGPT + Canva + Google Docs. We're faster, more integrated, better output.

> Strategic: Zapier, Make, n8n (no-code automation). Different positioning, but they could build something similar.

> Real challenge: Notion AI, GitHub Copilot, Cursor IDE. They're using similar models. But they're not focused on founder validation workflows.

> **We win by being laser-focused on founding journey, not being a general tool.**"

### **Q: How do you get to 10K paying subscribers by month 12?**
> "Distribution plan:
> - **Month 1:** Product Hunt launch. Target: 1K signups (10% convert to paid = 100).
> - **Month 2-3:** Organic growth + Twitter presence + founder community. 3K signups, 300 paid.
> - **Month 4-6:** Accelerator partnerships (YC, Techstars) — white-label offer, agency model. 5K signups, 1K paid.
> - **Month 7-12:** Paid acquisition (Google Ads: 'business plan generator'). Scale to 10K paid.

> It's aggressive but not impossible. Copy.ai hit 1M users in first year. We're not trying to be that, just 10K paying."

### **Q: What if APIs break? What if Gemini changes pricing?**
> "API risk is real. Mitigation:
> 1. We can swap Gemini for Claude or GPT-4. Multi-model support is on the roadmap.
> 2. Claude API is actually more expensive, but it exists.
> 3. If Gemini pricing goes 10x, our unit economics break. But that's a business risk, not a product risk. We'd pass on costs to users or raise prices.
> 4. We're building an internal LLM roadmap for Year 2 (fine-tuned models for specific agents).

> For now: single-model reliance is a risk we're taking."
