## **Key Investor Questions & Answers**

### **Q: Why do you need $200K in engineering if your product is already built?**

A: "The product handles the first 6 modules (Research, Branding, Landing, Feasibility, Shadow Board) at launch quality. But beta users are asking for automation: 'Post this to Twitter for me', 'Email my list with this sequence', 'Schedule my content calendar'.

That's a **new architecture**: Oauth integrations, scheduling queues, publishing APIs, analytics feedback loops. That's 6-8 months of engineering work for 2 people. We could do it with 1, but timeline doubles. Speed to market matters."

---

### **Q: Aren't Gemini API costs going to crush your margins if you scale?**

A: "Short answer: No. Long answer:

1. **We have volume leverage.** At 10K users (Month 12), we're likely top 1,000 customers by spend. Gemini will offer volume discounts.
2. **We can reduce API spend per user.** Today we call API 5x/month per user. With caching + result reuse, that drops to 2-3x/mo.
3. **We have optionality.** Claude API is 30% cheaper. GPT-4 is available. We're not hostage to Gemini.
4. **We can float price.** If API costs spike, we float to Pro tier ($15-20/mo). LTV still holds because margin is huge ($12 margin even at $20).

The real risk: **API pricing goes 10x** (very unlikely). Then we need to re-evaluate. But that's 2-3 years out because Gemini is competing aggressively."

---

### **Q: How do you compete with ChatGPT + Canva + Figma for free?**

A: "They don't compete with us. They're **component tools**. We're a **workflow tool**.

**ChatGPT:** Write copy for me → you get text. Now what? You still need design, landing page, lead capture, analytics.

**Canva:** Design a logo → you get PNG. Now what? You still need to validate the brand, build landing page, GTM.

**Forze:** 'Validate my founder-market fit' → you get research + brand + landing page + GTM strategy + financial projections.

The comparison is: Would you rather spend $0 + 30 hours with ChatGPT, or $9 + 5 minutes with Forze?

For a founder, time is **the** scarce resource. We're selling time, not pixels."

---

### **Q: What if AI training data cuts stop? What if copyright lawsuits break the industry?**

A: "Real risk. Real answer:

1. **We're using Gemini API**, not training our own models. If Gemini gets sued, Google pays—we don't.
2. **Our risk is model availability**, not legal liability.
3. **If Gemini breaks, we switch.** We're not locked to any model. We built agent abstraction layer specifically for this.
4. **Worst case:** We move to Claude or GPT-4 (more expensive, but possible). Margins shrink but don't disappear.

I'm not cavalier about this. But betting against LLMs in 2026 is like betting against the internet in 2000. Risk exists, but macro trend is too strong."

---

### **Q: What's your unfair advantage? Why don't Notion, Figma, or Canva just copy this?**

A: "Great question. They could build this, but:

1. **Contextual agent orchestration is hard.** Requires deep LLM API integration, multi-step prompt engineering, error recovery loops. We spent 3 months perfecting retry logic + thinking mode tuning. Notion engineers don't know this space yet.

2. **Focus misalignment.** Notion is about note-taking. Figma is about design. Canva is about templates. Building founders-facing automation pulls their focus. We're laser-focused.

3. **Network effects lock-in.** By Month 12, if we have 10K users, each venture exists in *our* database with all its context. Switching costs are real. You don't want to regenerate from scratch.

4. **The Shadow Board feature** (3 AI personas stress-testing your idea) is genuinely defensible. It requires extended thinking + persona consistency. Really hard to replicate well.

The real threat isn't Notion. It's: Google building this natively into Gmail/Docs. Or Y Combinator building it for their portfolio. Those are the existential risks we watch."

---

### **Q: What's the capital efficiency? Why not bootstrap?**

A: "Bootstrapping would work, but:

1. **Speed to Series A is the goal**, not maximizing founder equity.
2. **Market window is 12 months.** In Year 2, every AI SaaS company will be doing some version of this. We need to be first.
3. **Hiring with equity is hard.** Engineers want ramen-profitable companies (broke) or funded startups. In between is tough.
4. **$500K lets us hit $1M ARR by Month 12** with proof of unit economics. **That's the Series A trigger.** 

Bootstrapped route: 24-30 months to $1M ARR. Funded route: 12 months. In a fast-moving market, 12 months is the difference between pole position and pack."
