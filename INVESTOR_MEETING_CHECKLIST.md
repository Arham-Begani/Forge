## **DURING THE MEETING**

### **Opening (2 minutes)**

**Handshake. Pleasantries. Then:**

> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm going to show you something real, because I know you're busy. In 5 minutes, I'll have an idea turned into a market-ready venture. Here's the problem we're solving..."

---

### **The Hook: Problem (1 minute)**

**Lead with FOUNDER PAIN:**

> "I watched my friend spend $20K and 3 months hiring a dev, designer, copywriter. By the time they launched, the market had moved. They're bootstrapping their way back from a bad launch.

> This happens to **15 million founders** every year. Each one pays $2K-5K. That's a $37.5 billion market.

> It's an unfairness tax on not being an engineer. We're here to eliminate it."

---

### **Solution (1-2 minutes)**

**Show the demo. Type quickly. Narrate what's happening:**

> "I'm going to type an idea: [pause] 'AI voice transcription for lawyers.'

> In 5 minutes, 6 agents working in parallel will build:
> - Market research
> - Brand identity
> - Landing page (live)
> - Go-to-market strategy
> - Financial projections
> - Board feedback from AI personas

> [LET IT RUN while you talk]

> The magic is that each agent sees what the others built. Marketing writes in the brand voice. Landing page uses brand colors. Feasibility accounts for competition risks found in research.

> No other tool does this integrated."

---

### **Traction (45 seconds)**

**Show screens. Click through:**

> "We're not pre-product. All 6 modules shipped. Real-time streaming. Live deployment. User feedback loop already running.

> Next step: scale what we have."

---

### **Market (30 seconds)**

> "$37.5 billion TAM. $4.5B serviceable market we can reach. Why now? Gemini 3.0 Agent Teams, post-recession founder urgency, AI credibility reset."

---

### **The Ask (1 minute)**

> "We're raising $500K seed to scale this to Series A readiness.

> $200K engineering builds automation (publish to social, email). $120K gets us to Product Hunt launch + paid acquisition. At month 12, we're at $1M+ ARR, cash-flow positive, Series A ready.

> We're not asking for a bet on AI. We're asking for a bet on founder pain that we can measure, quantify, and solve."

---

### **Close (15 seconds)**

> "I'd love to have you as an early believer. Are you interested in digging deeper?"

## **Likely Questions & Quick Answers**

### **Q: Why Gemini not GPT-4?**
**A:** "Agent Teams + thinking mode + 50% cheaper. We could switch anytime if it makes sense."

### **Q: What if API pricing breaks your model?**
**A:** "Volume discounts kick in at scale. We have opt-out clauses. Worst case, we raise Pro pricing. Margins still healthy."

### **Q: How do you compete with ChatGPT?**
**A:** "ChatGPT is a text generator. We're a workflow. You need research, design, landing page, lead capture, analytics. We're integrated."

### **Q: Why $500K not $250K or $1M?**
**A:** "$250K leaves us under-resourced. $1M is over-dilution. $500K hits the sweet spot: hire good people, fund GTM, reach profitability."

### **Q: What's your unfair advantage?**
**A:** "Contextual glue. Integrated agent orchestration. Shadow Board. Hard to replicate fast enough."

### **Q: Why should I believe you'll hit these numbers?**
**A:** "Unit economics are proven, not promised. [Show LTV:CAC, gross margin data, CAC payback.] We're not guessing — we're extrapolating from real behavior."

### **Q: What happens if you don't hit Series A metrics?**
**A:** "We've built a sustainable business. If we hit $500K ARR with 80%+ NRR, we don't *need* Series A. We'd be profitable. Series A just accelerates."

## **If They Ask for Diligence**

**Be ready with:**

1. **GitHub repo** (code quality, commit history)
2. **3-5 beta user testimonials** (prove demand)
3. **Actual API cost data** (unit economics verification)
4. **Cap table** (no surprises)
5. **IP assignment docs** (founder owns everything)

## **If They Say "Interesting, Let's Talk More"**

**Next steps:**
1. Email them INVESTOR_1PAGER.md within 1 hour
2. Schedule follow-up (72 hours)
3. Send term sheet expectations (SAFE, priced seed, convertible note — your preference)
4. Ask who else should see this (partner, LPs)

## **If They Say "Not For Us"**

**Graceful exit:**

> "I appreciate the time. What's the core concern? Help me understand." 

**If it's market:**
> "Fair. We're proving traction over next 3 months. Check back then?"

**If it's team:**
> "I get it. What would give you confidence?" [Listen. Address it or move on.]

**If it's product timing:**
> "Cool. We're talking to $X other investors. Happy to keep you posted."

## **What NOT to Say**

❌ "This is the Uber of startup validation" — investors hate comparisons
❌ "We'll definitely cap the market in Year 2" — unproven scale claims
❌ "Competitors can't catch up" — they always can
❌ "Our team is the best in the world" — let work speak
❌ "We have no weaknesses" — you look delusional
❌ "We'll probably exit at $1B" — too much hype, kills credibility

---

## **What TO Say**

✅ "Here's our unfair advantage and why it's hard to replicate"
✅ "We're seeing X% conversion, Y% retention in beta"
✅ "Here's what could go wrong and how we're mitigating it"
✅ "We're raising $500K to achieve these specific milestones"
✅ "This is what success looks like for you as an investor"
✅ "Let me show you the product"

## **The Meta-Pitch (If They Ask Why You)**

> "I'm not a famous founder. I'm not backed by Y Combinator. What I have is: (1) I understand founder pain because I hear it every day. (2) I built a real product that solves it. (3) I'm focused on execution, not hype.

> What I'm asking from you is: (1) capital to scale. (2) advice (optionally). (3) help with one partnership intro.

> I'm not asking you to believe in me. I'm asking you to believe in the 15 million founders who need this. We're the vehicle."

## **Emergency Mode (If Things Go Off Rails)**

**If you blank:**
> "Good question. Let me send you the detailed analysis on that. [Look at notes]. Actually, here's the short answer..."

**If they're skeptical:**
> "I get it. Here's what changed my mind. [Tell a story. Show data. Be human.]"

**If they push on pricing:**
> "What price would make you comfortable? Let's work backward from there."

**If they push on timeline:**
> "What milestones would have to happen for you to be ready in 3 months?"

**If they ask for your cap table:**
> "You're the first person asking, which I like. Here it is. [Show it.] Question for you: what's your red flag on it?"
